# Go

## AI-era hypothesis

Go is advantaged for agentic development because of simplicity, fast tooling, predictable structure, and production-grade deployment characteristics.

## Strengths

- Simple language model
- Fast compile/test loops
- Strong standard tooling
- Good concurrency model
- Simple deployment

## Weaknesses

- Limited expressiveness compared with richer type systems
- Weaker abstraction mechanisms
- Less formal reasoning potential than Rust, Haskell, OCaml, or Ada/SPARK

## Open questions

- Does simplicity outperform stronger type systems for AI-agent repair loops?
- Is Go’s constrained design a long-term advantage or ceiling?
